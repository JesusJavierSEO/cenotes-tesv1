About Homún

Homún: the cenote village

Homún is a small town in the heart of Yucatán, about 50 km southwest of Mérida and close to Chichén Itzá. Its extraordinary concentration of over 20 accessible cenotes makes it one of the most unique eco-tourism destinations in Mexico.

Unlike the crowded cenotes of Tulum or Valladolid, Homún's cenotes remain peaceful and authentic. Crystal-clear water at 24°C year-round, stalactites, tropical fish and tranquility.

The town is also home to Kampepem Hacienda, a 19th-century henequén estate included in several tour packages.

FAQ

About visiting Homún

What is there to do in Homún, Yucatán?
Homún is famous for its 20+ crystal-clear cenotes. You can swim in open and underground cenotes, visit the historic Kampepem Hacienda, ride ATVs, zip-line and enjoy regional buffet food. One of the most authentic eco-tourism destinations in Yucatán.
Is Homún close to Chichén Itzá?
Yes — just 45 km away, about 45 minutes by car. A perfect combination for the same day trip.
Can kids visit the cenotes?
Absolutely. Life vests are included and all tours are family-friendly. Cenote Santa Bárbara even has an elevator, making it ideal for seniors and people with reduced mobility.
What is the water temperature?
About 24°C (75°F) year-round — refreshing and very comfortable for swimming.

Homún, Yucatán: Yucatán's best-kept secret

Homún is a small municipality in the heart of the Yucatán Peninsula, about 50 km southwest of Mérida. Known locally as "the cenote village," it offers an extraordinary concentration of over 20 accessible sinkholes connected by the same underground cave network that runs beneath the entire peninsula.

If you're looking for things to do near Chichén Itzá or an alternative to the crowded cenotes of Tulum, Homún is the answer. Its proximity to major archaeological sites — 45 km from Chichén Itzá and 50 km from Mérida — makes it a natural complement to any Yucatán itinerary.
